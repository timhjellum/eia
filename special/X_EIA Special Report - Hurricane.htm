  • As of 10:00 a.m. Central Daylight Time, June 30, 2010, Hurricane Alex, the first named Atlantic Basin tropical cyclone of the 2010 season, is moving through the Gulf of Mexico, about 190 miles southeast of Brownsville, Texas. Its current maximum sustained wind speed is 80 MPH, and it is heading NW at 7 MPH. Current projections suggest it will makelandfall on the Mexican coast, south of the Rio Grande river, late on June 30 or early on July 1. These projections are subject to significant revision as the storm continues to develop.
  • Hurricane Alex does not currently appear to pose a major threat to offshore or onshore energy facilities. However, EIA will continue to monitor projections and revise this page as appropriate.
