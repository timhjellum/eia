Most U.S. petroleum imports come from the Persian Gulf (true or false)?

  1. True
  2. False

Next question >

< Previous question

INCORRECT CORRECT

Answer is False.

About half of U.S. crude oil and petroleum products net imports typically come from the Western Hemisphere (North, South, and Central America and the Caribbean including U.S. territories). The United States gets the largest share of net imports from Canada! Our next largest sources of crude oil and petroleum product imports are Venezuela and Saudi Arabia. In 2011, about 22% of our crude oil and petroleum product net imports came from the Persian Gulf countries of Bahrain, Iraq, Kuwait, Qatar, Saudi Arabia, and the United Arab Emirates.
