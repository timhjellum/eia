Energy-related carbon dioxide (CO2 ) emissions declined in 2011. Which of the following factors did not contribute to the 2011 decline in energy-related CO2 emissions?

  1. lower natural gas prices
  2. more use of renewable energy
  3. reduced electricity generation from coal
  4. a decline in the size of the U.S. economy

Next question >

< Previous question

INCORRECT CORRECT

Answer is a decline in the size of the U.S. economy.

After two years of declining carbon dioxide emissions (2008 and 2009) and one year of increasing emissions (2010), CO2 emissions fell in 2011. Unlike 2009, the 2011 decline occurred during a year of positive growth in the Gross Domestic Product (GDP). CO2 emissions in 2011 were 526 million metric tons, 9% below the 2005 level. See full report.

Source: U.S. Energy Information Administration, U.S. Energy-Related Carbon Dioxide Emissions, 2011 , released August 14, 2012.
