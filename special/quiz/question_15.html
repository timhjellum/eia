Electricity is called a secondary energy source because...

  1. it's our #2 source of energy
  2. it's not as important as oil and gas
  3. we get it from converting other energy sources
  4. all of the above

INCORRECT CORRECT

Answer is...we get it from converting other energy sources.

Electricity is a secondary energy source, or energy carrier, because we get it from the conversion of other sources of energy, such as coal, nuclear, and renewable energy. These are called primary energy sources.
