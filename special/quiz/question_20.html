The daily amount of energy used in the United States, per person, is about as much energy as....

  1. 100 watt lightbulb left on for 104 days straight
  2. 707 quarter-pound hamburgers
  3. Half a tank of gasoline (7 gallons)
  4. All of the above

INCORRECT CORRECT

Answer is all of the above.

The total amount of energy used in the United States includes homes, businesses, industry, and transportation. In 2011, each person in the United States used an average of 854,800 British thermal units (Btu) per day. Energy sources are expressed in different units, but their energy content can be compared using British thermal units (Btu). Food calories can also be converted to Btu.
