Historical/Selected Significant U.S. Energy Disruptions
Released: August 31, 2010

Hurricane Season 2010
The National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration’s (NOAA) 2010 Atlantic Hurricane Season Outlook predicted that the Atlantic basin will likely experience above-normal tropical weather activity during the 2010 hurricane season (June 1 – November 30). Hurricane Earl, the season's second major hurricane, was the first to raise concerns about its potential to cause significant energy disruption.

Historical Disruptions
In previous years, the U.S. Energy Information Administration has tracked and published the following special reports of selected significant energy disruption events:

  • Ike — Landfall September 13, 2008
  • Gustav — Landfall September 1, 2008
  • Rita — Landfall September 23, 2005
  • Katrina — August 25, 2005
